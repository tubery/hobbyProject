My favourite games as of

My first experience with video games was when I was a kid watching my cousins play ocarina of time on the nintendo 64. From that day I would go over and play games as much as I could. This has become my favourite pasttime till this day.

While I have played many games over thousands of hours I have never created a concrete list of my favourite games as there are too many. As I am not a writer or game reviewer these ratings will be mostly based on how they impacted me.

When I am playing games the main areas that stand out to me are listed below.

  • Feeling & Gameplay - How it feels to control the character and how much fun the gameplay loop is.
  • Story & Characters - How much the story affects me and how interesting the characters are.
  • Music - When I listen to game soundtracks am I able to recall the game ,the scenes, and the feelings
  • Graphics & Setting - This is the game world and how its presented.
  • Replayability - How many times can I play the game without getting bored.

WARNING! SPOILERS AHEAD!

10. Destiny 2

Looter Shooter King

My favourite looter shooter. Sometimes games are too complex depending on my state of mind. When I want casual fun where my time is rewarded I boot up looter games like Diablo 3 or when I want to shoot things Destiny 2. This game has shooting mechanics that are like no other. I am a hand cannon fanatic I hunt for the best feeling ones and keep many in my inventory.

9. Guild wars 2

Best MMORPG

The stand out feature for this game for me is the WvWvW or WvW (WorldvsWorldvsWorld). When I bought this game in 2012 I did not complete the story quest instead went into WvW. From there I spent around 95% of my time in this game mode when playing this game.

There aren't many games that have this feature the only one I can think of is ESO (The Elder scrolls online). The threeway large scale battles involving different servers is unique and so much fun. The combat and community make this truly unique as each server has its own identity, people and ways to play.

8. Elden Ring

Favourite Soulslike

My first foray into the souls like genre that wasn't sekiro. I would not classify sekiro as a souls like game due to its aggresive nature and the parrying mechanic. In this game utilising the dodge roll and waiting for an oppurtunity sets it apart.

The strongest parts of this game is the music and combat. The amount of playstyles, weapons, spells and skills make this game replayable and enjoyable. The main bosses each unique encounters with their own music, Morgott, Mohg, Godrick, Malenia, Radahn, Rennala, Godfrey, Radagon. It is addicting learning how to overcome a challegning encounter.

7. Sekiro

Best combat ever

For me this is the game with the best combat i have played. The parrying system is so satisfying and rewarding that no other game has come close to it. The strongest parts of this game is the combat and visuals. The enviroments where boss battles are set and the battles themselves allows this to be put in sixth place.

6. Call of duty 4 | Call of duty MW2 | Halo 3

Favourite First person shooters

Every day after school I would hop on my XBOX 360 and load up either, fifa10, cod4 or halo 3. Call of duty 4 ,MW2 and halo 3 are in this spot due to the amount of fun and hours spent laughing and playing with friends. For a game which is simply shooting people it held me over for 3 years.

5. The Witcher 3

Favourite Modern RPG

My first CD Projekt Red game. As a person who enjoys as much freedom as possible from games this game cemented my patronage for this company. Both cyberpunk 2077 and the witcher 3 hit my feelings hard and kept me engaged with the story and its characters. Also team Yennefer.

While many were not impressed with the combat I love the fluidity and responsiveness of the controls and its simplicity. I prefer games which do not require use of many skills or items mainly focusing on a few strong skills, in this case quen, step dodging and light attacks.

Team yen is the only way. The base game in addition to its two dlcs hearts of stone, and blood and wine give this game incredible value. They could be sold as three separte games. The stories makes me invested in these characters wanting the best for each of them and trying to get the best ending.

Cloak and dagger, Steel for humans, Silver for monsters, Battle theme, Yennefer theme, Geralt of rivia, Toussaint, Street music, etc... I could go on and on about the soundtracks but there are too many. The soundtrack is perfectly suited for this world and every situation they are used in.

4. Mass Effect Trilogy

Best Trilogy

My second foray into bioware games. For movies Lord of the rings is the best trilogy and for video games I believe it is the mass effect trilogy. The persistance across the games, the choices, gameplay, and story make this the best.

The strongest point for the game is the story, choices and characters. Being a bioware game you have many characters whose lives are intertwined with the story, eachother and your choices. Once again proving their writing skills.

3. The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

Modding Heaven

My second elder scrolls game after oblivion. While I was debating putting oblivion here I chose skyrim as I have more hours as I have continously returned to skyrim through modding.

My first choice for RPGs is always a human mage. In this game the magic schools allowed me to roleplay as a mage wielding many forms of magic. Through modding the game it adds immersion and replayability. For vanilla skyrim I have around 400 hours and with modding I have around 3000 hours. Every couple of months I will return to this game install new modpacks and play through the game.

Enderal the only full scale mod I loved. This mod deserves its on position as it set the bar for mods and it is one of a kind currently.

2. Dragon age: Origins

RPG measuring bar

My first bioware game and introduction to choices and consequences in gaming. My first game with romancable characters and companions with personalities.

This game set the bar very high for future RPGs. I will always remember the party I used for this game, Alistair, Leliana, Morrigan and my grey warden. The choices and interactions with companions are incredible before this I had only played linear games. The freedom to choose different paths for the story has made me a bioware fan.

Ive never liked tactical gameplay in dragon age and Ive played every one. The game is so good in other areas it doesnt bother me as much. However with the annoucement to move away from tactical for dragon age 4 I am excited.

Morrigan my first video game love. From witch of the wilds who only cared about herself to a loving wife and mother. Leliana a bard(assassin) escaping her path and finding a new life in the chantry. Alistair a grey warden and reluctant king. There are too many loved characters but biowares strength writing characters is incredible.

The amount of choices, races, classes and different companions makes this game incredibly replayable. I only play human in RPGs but for people who don't mind playing other races it adds extra replayability.

1. Read Dead Redemption 2 (RDR2)

The GOAT

The GOAT. Currently the greatest game of all time in my opinion. Every aspect is perfect, feeling, graphics, music, story, characters, fun and replayability. I will always remember Arthur Morgan and his journey. He is the greatest video game protagonist.

When playing this game I am incredibly immersed and feel like the protagonist arthur morgan. Everytime I think about this game I think about the story and how much I love the characters. The single action mechanic is incredible especially using an xbox controller with the trigger buttons, it feels like im more invovled with the gun.

From thief to a thief with a heart of gold depending on how you play. But one thing is always true arthur cares about his family Characters. A game were you care about every character in the main camp is incredible I spent so much time at camp listening and talking to the gang members

American venom, You're my brother, Unshaken, Thats the way it is, See the fire in your eyes, . Everytime I hear these soundtracks all the feelings, goosebumps come flowing back. Incredible music tied with memorable moments and cinematic visuals.

The world is so big and detailed it is well positioned for multiple play throughs and atleast two if wanting to see experience between low and high honor arthur.

These are the games of the top of my head Im sure there are far more games or games I havent played which deserve a spot however I am limited by time and platform availability. Thank you for reading.
