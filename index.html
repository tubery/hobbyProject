My favourite games of all time as of

My first experience with video games was when I was a young kid watching my cousins play ocarina of time on the nintendo 64. From that day I would go over and play games as much as I could. This has become my favourite pasttime till this day.

While I have played many games over thousands of hours I have never created a concrete list of my favourite games as there are too many.

Disclaimer. I am not a writer or game reviewer. The criteria listed below is loose and not very defined as the games impact is what I value the most however all of these games score high in these areas as they are my top 10.

  • Feeling & Gameplay
  • Story & Characters
  • Music
  • Graphics & Setting
  • Replayability

9. Most time spent

Guild wars 2

I have many MMOs ranging from runescape, World of warcraft, Elder scrolls online, and Archeage. However there is only one MMO I always return to and check in on when I have time is GW2. This horizontal progression means I do not have to stay commited to the game and hunt for better gear everytime an update comes out. Every class is unique and fun to play. I have all classes on my account however my main class is an elementalist as it was my first and I enjoy mages.

weirdly enough I do not play PVE in this game only PVP and WvW. Mainly world v wolrd I have not even completed the main story instead spent years doing WvW the combat and community have made this game unique and different everyday.

8. Arena shooter

Halo 3

My love for Bungie started here. Back then the PS3 was not as big for some reason. Everyone had an xbox and xbox live. Halo 3 was particularly big. The multiplayer, forge, gamemodes, vehicles and maps had enough variety that allowed me to play for the entirety of my school life.

During this time I would also watch the MLG series with my friends the first esports I watched.

7. First Soulslike

Elden Ring

My first foray into the souls like genre that wasn't sekiro. I would not classify sekiro as a souls like game due to its aggresive nature and the parrying mechanic. In this game utilising the dodge roll and waiting for an oppurtunity sets it apart.

While the story is not much the huge amount of lore within the game gives it much depth which I found using videos online since it requires ingame reading, I only watched the cinematics.

The strongest parts of this game is the music and combat. The amount of playstyles, weapons, spells and skills make this game replayable and enjoyable. The main bosses each unique encounters with their own music, Morgott, Mohg, Godrick, Malenia, Radahn, Rennala, Godfrey, Radagon.

6. Best combat ever

Sekiro

For me this is the game with the best combat i have played. The parrying system is so satisfying and rewarding that no other game has come close to it. The strongest parts of this game is the combat and visuals. The enviroments where boss battles are set and the battles themselves allows this to be put in sixth place.

5. Intro to Modern RPG

The Witcher 3

My first CD Projekt Red game. As a person who enjoys as much freedom as possible from games this game cemented my patronage for this company. Both cyberpunk 2077 and the witcher 3 hit my feelings hard and kept me engaged with the story and its characters. Also team Yennefer.

Feeling & Gameplay- While many were not impressed with the combat I love the fluidity and responsiveness of the controls and its simplicity. I prefer games which do not require use of many skills or items mainly focusing on a few strong skills, in this case quen, step dodging and light attacks.

Story & Characters - Team yen is the only way. The base game in addition to its two dlcs hearts of stone, and blood and wine give this game incredible value. They could be sold as three separte games. The stories and characters

Music - lelellelle , Cloak and dagger, Steel for humans, Silver for monsters, Battle theme, Yennefer theme, Geralt of rivia, Toussaint, Street music,

Graphics & Setting - The witcher 3, cyberpunk 2077 and red dead redemption 2 are the graphic benchmarks for me personally.

Replayability - The amount of choices make this game replayable to see different outcomes.

4. Best Trilogy

Mass Effect Trilogy

My second foray into bioware games. For movies Lord of the rings is the best trilogy and for video games I believe it is the mass effect trilogy. The persistance across the games, the choices, gameplay, and story make this the best.

Feeling & Gameplay- I have always loved first and third person shooters so when I found out bioware had a game after playing dragon age I quickly went to gamestop and purchased these games. As with every RPG my goto is a human mage and here the mage are the biotics. Playing as a caster and shooter is fun the effects, sound and visuals are improved upon each iteration of the game. The cover shooting isnt new but its solid.

Story & Characters - The strongest point for the game is the story, choices and characters. Being a bioware game you have many characters whose lives are intertwined with the story, eachother and your choices. Once again proving their writing skills.

Music - Leaving earth, Suicide mission,

Graphics & Setting - With the release of the anniversary edition it is has become more playable however even the old versions hold up well. Setting in a futuristic world where large distance travel is normal.

Replayability - The amount of choices classes and different companions makes this game incredibly replayable.

3. Modding Heaven

The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

My second elder scrolls game after oblivion. While I was debating putting oblivion here I chose skyrim as I have more hours as I have continously returned to skyrim through modding.

My first choice for RPGs is always a human mage. In this game the magic schools allowed me to roleplay as a magic wielding many forms of magic. Through modding the game became immersive and replayable. For vanilla skyrim I have around 400 hours and with modding I have around 3000 hours. Every couple of months I will return to this game install new modpacks and play through the game.

Enderal the only full scale mod I loved. This mod deserves its on position as it set the bar for mods and it is one of a kind currently.

Feeling & Gameplay - Using two hands for magic is one of skyrims unique mechanics as there arent many first person magic games. This allowed me to be any type of mage I wanted to be. I enjoyed playing as an archer and sword and board. The amount of rereleases show the fun factor of the game.

Story & Characters - Nothing too revolutionary here as you are a weak person at the begining getting more powerful to defeat the big bad at the end. There is more lore throughout the world if explored.

Music - When the skyrim trailer launched it became instantly iconic. Everyone knows when the chants start what theme is being played.

Graphics & Setting - The graphics were good for a game released in its time. It has been rereleased so many times and hasn't changed much.

Replayability - The world is so big with so many quests it requires multiple play throughs. There are different ways of playing such as stealth, magic, melee and more.

2. RPG measuring bar

Dragon age: Origins

My first bioware game and introduction to choices and consequences in gaming. My first game with romancable characters and companions with personalities.

This game set the bar very high for future RPGs. I will always remember the party I used for this game, Alistair, Leliana, Morrigan and my grey warden. The choices and interactions with companions are incredible before this I had only played linear games. The freedom to choose different paths for the story has made me a bioware fan.

Feeling & Gameplay- FOR FERELDEN AND FOR THE GREY WARDENNSSSSSSS - Alistair. Ive never liked tactical gameplay in dragon age and Ive played every one. The game is so good in other areas it doesnt bother me as much. However with the annoucement to move away from tactical for dragon age 4 I am excited.

Story & Characters - Oh Morrigan my first video game love. From witch of the wilds who only cared about herself to a loving wife and mother. Leliana a bard(assassin) escaping her path and finding a new life in the chantry. Alistair a grey warden and reluctant king. There are too many loved characters but biowares strength writing characters is incredible.

Music - Chantries hubris and main theme. While the music wasnt too stand out consisting of orchestral themes the main theme which plays throughout the game and the chantries hubris during the final mission is incredible memorable.

Graphics & Setting - The graphics were incredible back then and still hold up today with good looking models and enviroments.

Replayability - The amount of choices, races, classes and different companions makes this game incredibly replayable. I only play human in RPGs but for people who don't mind playing other races it adds extra replayability.

1. The GOAT

Read Dead Redemption 2 (RDR2)

The GOAT. Currently the greatest game of all time in my opinion. Every aspect is perfect, feeling, graphics, music, story, characters, fun and replayability.

I will always remember Arthur Morgan and his journey. He is the greatest video game protagonist.

Feeling & Gameplay - When playing this game I am incredibly immersed and feel like the protagonist arthur morgan. Everytime I think about this game I think about the story and how much I love the characters. The single action mechanic is incredible especially using an xbox controller with the trigger buttons, it feels like im more invovled with the gun.

Story & Characters - From thug to thug with a heart of gold depending on how you play. But one thing is always true arthur cares about his family Characters. A game were you care about every character in the main camp is incredible I spent so much time at camp listening and talking to the gang members

Music - American venom, You're my brother, Unshaken, Thats the way it is, See the fire in your eyes, . Everytime I hear these soundtracks all the feelings, goosebumps come flowing back. Incredible music tied with memorable moments and cinematic visuals.

Graphics & Setting - On my old rig which has a 5700xt this game looks incredible. The characters are all detailed the world is crisp and sharp.

Replayability - The world is so big and detailed it is well positioned for multiple play throughs and atleast two if wanting to see experience between low and high honor arthur.
